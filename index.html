Let's Revolutionize Worldwide Development

How?

Upwards crowdsources and clusters data to elicit more effective policymaking and development efforts

Upwards brings together data from around the world on past and ongoing developmental efforts of all types and scopes. Upwards then performs state of the art machine learning algorithms to uncover latent structures and similarities between these efforts. With this knowledge base of past experience and insight, local leaders, policymakers, and changemakers can be much more successful in solving the global developental problems at hand.

Learn

Get data about both completed and ongoing efforts similar to the one you are working on. We use clustering algorithms to find projects that worked on a similar task and occurred in the closest initial developmental context (economic state, population dynamics, social capital, etc) to your own. Learn about what kinds of efforts were attempted and their successes/failutres, so that you can choose the best approach for your project

Share

Share data and details about a developmental effort you are involved in so that other people can make use of this experience and reach out to you. In doing so, you open up your project to recieve feedback and experience from Upwards users, and also gain the ability to access premium data sources and analysis.

Get Involved

Find ongoing developmental projects nearby your location which you can contribute to, or find people who can contribute to your own effort. Scholars from across the world can provide guidance and insight on niche developmental efforts.

Learn more about the concept

In a village in Northern Tanzania, a small internationally based NGO leads the development of a community-based irrigation solution by working with farmers that will allow ample and equitable water distribution. Meanwhile in the Sarawak district of Malaysia, a bureaucratic officer looks for ways to reform the tax department so that citizens might regain faith in the tax officers and pay taxes voluntarily. In India, a cabinet minister of the state of Gujarat is trying to figure out economic policies that will attract foreign capital investment while creating safeguards against extractive corporations and capital volatility.

All the nations of the world, thousands of provincial districts, tens of thousands of small towns, have collectively tried out hundreds of thousands of policies and implementation efforts for achieving various socioeconomic development goals. These efforts, originating both from within and outside the government, range from infrastructure development to the education of women, with each one leading to unique results.

It’s time to bring this data together, so that change-makers spread across the world can learn from each other and have access to real data on what works and what doesn’t. By bringing together the insights and wisdom of developmental efforts into one platform and uncovering latent similarities in this data through statistical analysis, we can help leaders and change-makers make better decisions that are more likely to engender positive change.
